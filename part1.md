‚Ä¢ Natural privacy has been eroded by digital technologies that remove physical barriers to information access.
‚Ä¢ Normative privacy depends on norms, roles, and expectations within a given context, such as education or healthcare.
‚Ä¢ Contextual Integrity focuses on actors, attributes, transmission principles, and context.

‚Ä¢ Phone data breaches violate normative privacy even if data was technically accessible.
‚Ä¢ A professor sharing alumni emails may breach contextual norms despite benign intentions.
‚Ä¢ Contextual Integrity assesses whether information flows align with contextual expectations.

Evaluation / Critique
‚Ä¢ Consequentialism may downplay privacy violations if benefits outweigh harms.
‚Ä¢ Deontology emphasises duties to respect informational autonomy.
‚Ä¢ Virtue ethics examines trustworthiness and respect.

Conclusion
‚Ä¢ Contextual Integrity bridges natural and normative privacy by grounding privacy analysis in social norms.
‚Ä¢ It provides a flexible yet structured framework for evaluating modern privacy dilemmas.
